Strategic Plan — 250 Acres, Wilmington IL

01 · Rotation
Rotation Structure
  1. Maintain strict corn–soy rotation. Never go continuous corn at current prices.
  2. Target 60/40 corn/soy split (150 ac / 100 ac) as baseline.
  3. Shift to 50/50 when soy:corn price ratio exceeds 2.5:1.
  4. Consider winter wheat double-crop soy on 20–30 ac as pest management.
02 · Selling windows
Marketing Discipline
  1. Never sell at October harvest. You leave 11–13% on the table.
  2. Corn: sell incrementally Feb–June, 100% done by July 4.
  3. Soy: sell incrementally Apr–August, heaviest Jun–Jul.
  4. Watch SA crop Feb–Mar: large SA = sell earlier; small SA = hold for summer.
03 · Risk
Risk Management
  1. Revenue Protection crop insurance at February projected price.
  2. Hedge 25–30% of expected production with CBOT futures pre-harvest.
  3. Track basis at local elevators. Will County corn basis strengthens Apr–May.
  4. Keep 10% unpriced as upside optionality for drought-year spikes.

Price safety net

What your crop is worth with and without price protection
What this shows. You've got grain coming off the field. Instead of just selling at whatever the market gives you, you can set up a deal with your broker that guarantees a minimum price no matter how far the market drops — a floor. In exchange, gains above a ceiling go to the broker, not you.

Think of it like crop insurance, but for price instead of weather. The blue dashed line below is what you'd get with no protection. The solid green line is what you'd get with the floor-and-ceiling deal in place. Drag the slider to see how the two compare at any price.

How to read the chart. Pick a price with the slider. The dashed blue dot is what you'd make with no protection — straight market price. The solid green dot is what you'd make with the safety net in place. When the green dot sits above the blue one, the floor is kicking in (prices fell, net caught you). When the green dot is below the blue one, the ceiling is active (market rallied past the cap, broker keeps the extra). When they're on top of each other, the market is in the middle zone and the net isn't doing anything — which is the most common outcome.

The brown dots on the bottom of the chart are the prices where your sale plan automatically sells chunks of the crop. If the market hits one of those, you're selling that tranche at a better price than the floor — which is the whole point.
The safety-net deal expires Nov 20, 2026 (corn) / Oct 23, 2026 (soy). Numbers shown are at-expiry outcomes — early behavior differs slightly. Not financial advice; talk to your broker before setting this up.
